Overview

Who We Are

CALDON™ is a private property development firm delivering residential and commercial projects across New South Wales. We partner with select investors to identify, acquire, and deliver development projects. You provide the capital. We provide the expertise. You retain full ownership of the asset.

This is not a fund or a syndicate. Your capital is not pooled with other investors. Each project is structured individually, with you as the owner and CALDON™ as your development partner.

We handle site identification, feasibility analysis, design, approvals, construction oversight, and project completion. All core functions—architecture, project management, construction management, quantity surveying, and DA administration—are delivered in-house.

Who This Is For

Business owners, professionals, and family offices with capital to deploy but limited time to manage a development themselves.

Investors who understand property fundamentals and want exposure to development margins without operational complexity.

Those seeking a hands-off role with decision-making authority at key milestones.

This is not for:

Investors seeking passive, pooled fund structures. Those looking for guaranteed returns or short-term speculation. Anyone unable to commit capital for the full project duration (typically 18–36 months).

Minimum equity contribution is typically $500,000 per project, varying by scale and finance structure.

Why This Model Exists

Most investors face a binary choice: take a passive role with limited upside, or take an active role without the expertise to execute well. Development requires planning knowledge, construction experience, consultant networks, and council relationships that take years to build.

We bridge that gap through a structured, execution-led partnership model. You bring capital and decision-making authority. We bring execution capability and accountability. Our fee is typically a fraction of the value created through yield uplift, approval optimisation, and execution certainty.

We become your development team. You don't need to source sites, negotiate with councils, manage consultants, or supervise builders. That's our job.

Process

From Strategy to Completion

Our process is structured around staged commitments and clear decision points. You are never locked in without understanding what comes next—and you may exit at any stage with full documentation.

1

Initial Conversation

Confidential discussion to understand your investment objectives, risk appetite, timeline, and preferred structures. No cost. No obligation.

2

Strategy & Site Identification

Tailored investment strategy. Site identification across our target markets. Preliminary feasibility and market analysis.

3

Option & Feasibility

Site secured through option agreement where possible. Concept design. Detailed feasibility with conservative assumptions. Your decision to proceed or exit.

4

Detailed design and engineering. Site acquisition. Full documentation for development application.

5

Approvals & Tender

DA lodgement and management. Construction certificate. Builder tender and contract negotiation. Fixed-price contracts targeted.

6

Construction & Completion

Client-side project management throughout construction. Progress monitoring, quality oversight, variation assessment, defect management. Handover of completed asset.

Timeline

Total project duration is typically 20–36 months from engagement to completion, depending on project scale and complexity. Simpler projects trend shorter; larger developments trend longer.

Fees

Our project fee covers all in-house services and is paid in stages tied to completed milestones. Fees are transparent, agreed upfront, and structured so we are incentivised to deliver efficiently—not to extend timelines or inflate scope.

Investor costs (separate from our fee) include site acquisition, construction, council contributions, legal, accounting, and finance costs where applicable. Our fee structure is designed to align incentives, not to maximise billable scope.

Detailed fee schedules are provided during initial discussions and formalised prior to engagement.

Risk & Capital Protection

How We Protect Your Capital

Development carries risk. Our role is to identify, quantify, and manage those risks—not eliminate them entirely, but structure projects so that downside is understood and contained.

Option Structures

Where possible, sites are secured through option agreements before significant capital is committed. If feasibility doesn't stack up, you walk away with limited exposure.

Staged Commitment

Each phase requires your approval before proceeding. You review deliverables, assess risks, and make informed decisions at each milestone. No automatic progression.

Conservative Feasibility

We model with realistic assumptions—not optimistic sales prices or aggressive timelines. Projects that only work on best-case scenarios don't proceed.

Fixed-Price Construction

We target fixed-price building contracts where possible, transferring construction cost risk to the builder before you commit to the build phase.

Key Risks

Planning: DA refusal or conditions affecting feasibility. Managed through council pre-lodgement, compliant designs, and realistic yield expectations.

Construction: Cost overruns, delays, or builder failure. Managed through fixed-price contracts, quality builder selection, and active monitoring.

Market: End values lower than projected. Managed through conservative valuations, demand validation, and exit flexibility.

Finance: Inability to secure or service debt. Managed through pre-approval discussions, conservative LVRs, and cash flow planning.

Timeline: Project takes longer than planned. Managed through realistic scheduling, contingency allowances, and proactive management.

Development isn't risk-free. But the difference between a good outcome and a poor one usually comes down to how risks are identified early and managed throughout.

Returns

Well-structured property development typically targets 15–30% return on equity over an 18–36 month project cycle—compared with 3–7% annual yields from passive property investment.

Returns are indicative and depend on individual project characteristics. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Development returns are driven by planning uplift, cost control, and execution discipline—not speculation or leverage.
