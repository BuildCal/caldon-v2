Executive Summary

Overview

What We Do

CALDON™ partners with investors who want property development returns without the operational burden of managing a project themselves. You provide the capital. We provide the expertise. You retain ownership of the asset.

We identify development opportunities, manage feasibility, coordinate design and approvals, oversee construction, and deliver completed assets to investors. All core functions—architecture and design, project management, construction management, quantity surveying, and DA administration—are handled in-house by our expert team. Our role is to handle everything that sits between finding a site and handing you the keys to a finished building.

This model works because most investors don't have the time, relationships, or technical knowledge to run a development themselves—but they understand property and want exposure to development margins without the operational complexity.

The Opportunity

Market Context

Why Development Partnership

Property development offers returns that passive investment rarely matches. But most investors face a binary choice: take a passive role with limited upside, or take an active role without the expertise to execute well.

The Challenge for Investors

Business owners, professionals, and family offices often have capital to deploy but limited time. They understand property's fundamentals—tangible assets, rental income, capital growth—but development feels like a different discipline entirely.

Development requires planning expertise, construction knowledge, consultant management, and relationships that take years to build. The learning curve is steep, and the cost of mistakes is high.

Where CALDON™ Fits

We bridge the gap between passive investment and active development. You bring capital and decision-making authority. We bring the technical capability, relationships, and execution experience.

This isn't a fund or a syndicate where your capital is pooled with others. You own your project outright. Our fee is for the service of managing your development—not for a share of your asset.

What Development Returns Look Like

Investment Type Typical Returns Time Horizon Your Role
Established residential 3-5% yield + capital growth Hold indefinitely Passive owner
Commercial property 5-7% yield + growth Hold indefinitely Passive owner
Property development 15-30% return on equity 18-36 months Capital partner

Returns are indicative and depend on individual project characteristics. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Development returns are driven by planning uplift, cost control, and execution discipline—not speculation or leverage.

The Process

How It Works

From First Conversation to Completed Asset

Our process is designed around staged commitments and clear decision points. You're never locked in without understanding exactly what comes next—and you can walk away at any stage with full documentation.

01

Initial Conversation

We start with a confidential discussion to understand your investment objectives, risk appetite, timeline, and preferred structures.

No cost. No obligation. Typically 45-60 minutes.

02

Strategy & Site Identification

Based on your criteria, we develop a tailored investment strategy and begin identifying suitable sites with market analysis and preliminary site reports.

Deliverables: Investment strategy brief, site reports, preliminary feasibility.

03

Option & Feasibility

We negotiate vendor terms and secure the opportunity through an option agreement. This limits your exposure while we conduct detailed feasibility.

Deliverables: Option agreement, concept plans, detailed feasibility, risk register.

04

Design & Acquisition

With feasibility confirmed, we proceed to detailed design and site acquisition including full documentation and engineering.

Deliverables: DA documentation, engineering drawings, settlement on site.

05

Approvals & Tender

DA lodgement and management through to determination. Once approved, we prepare tender documentation and negotiate builder contracts.

Deliverables: DA approval, CC application, builder contract.

06

Construction & Completion

Client-side project management throughout the build—progress monitoring, quality oversight, variation assessment, and defect management.

Deliverables: Completed asset, occupation certificate, depreciation schedule.

Investor Portal

Every investor gets secure access to our online portal. Track project progress in real-time, view updates, access all deal and project documentation, and communicate directly with your project team—all in one place, available 24/7.

Risk Management

Managing Downside

How We Protect Your Capital

Development carries risk. Our job is to identify, quantify, and manage those risks—not eliminate them entirely, but structure projects so that downside is understood and contained.

Option Structures

Where possible, we secure sites through option agreements before you commit significant capital. If feasibility doesn't stack up, you walk away with limited exposure.

Staged Commitment

Each phase requires your approval before proceeding. You review deliverables, assess risks, and make informed decisions at each milestone.

Conservative Feasibility

We model with realistic assumptions—not optimistic sales prices or aggressive timelines. Projects that only work on best-case scenarios don't proceed.

Fixed Price Construction

We target fixed-price building contracts where possible, transferring construction cost risk to the builder before you commit.

Key Risks in Development

Risk What It Means How We Manage It
Planning DA refused or conditions affect feasibility Council pre-lodgement, compliant designs, realistic yield
Construction Costs exceed budget, delays, builder failure Fixed-price contracts, quality builder selection, monitoring
Market End values lower than projected Conservative valuations, demand validation, exit flexibility
Finance Inability to secure or service debt Pre-approval discussions, conservative LVRs, cash flow planning
Timeline Project takes longer than planned Realistic timelines, contingency, proactive management

Development isn't risk-free. But the difference between a good outcome and a poor one usually comes down to how risks are identified early and managed throughout.

Structures

Ownership Options

How Investments Are Structured

The right ownership structure depends on your personal circumstances—tax position, asset protection goals, estate planning, and how you intend to hold or exit the asset. We work with your accountant and solicitor to ensure the structure is optimised from day one.

Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV)

A company established specifically for the project. Clean structure with limited liability. Can be wound up or sold post-completion.

Suitable for: Single projects, joint ventures, clean exit strategies.

Company

Property held in a proprietary limited company. Flat 25-30% tax rate. No CGT discount, but profits can be retained or distributed as franked dividends.

Suitable for: Multiple projects, reinvestment of profits, asset protection.

Discretionary (Family) Trust

Flexibility to distribute profits to beneficiaries in lower tax brackets. 50% CGT discount available. Strong asset protection benefits.

Suitable for: Families with varying tax positions, estate planning.

Unit Trust

Fixed entitlements based on unit holdings. Suitable for multiple investors with defined shares. 50% CGT discount available to individual unit holders.

Suitable for: Joint ventures, multiple investors, defined profit splits.

Self-Managed Super Fund

Property held within your SMSF. Concessional tax rates (15% income, 10% CGT if held 12+ months). Strict compliance requirements.

Suitable for: Long-term hold strategies, retirement planning.

Hybrid Structures

Combinations such as a unit trust with a corporate trustee, or an SPV held by a family trust. Tailored to specific circumstances.

Suitable for: Complex requirements, optimising tax and asset protection.

Working With Your Advisers

We don't provide tax or legal advice—that's the role of your accountant and solicitor. What we do is coordinate with your advisory team to ensure the development structure aligns with your broader financial strategy.

Before engaging on a project, we recommend discussing with your advisers:

The most appropriate ownership structure for your circumstances
Finance options and how debt will be secured
Cash flow requirements and timing of capital calls
Exit strategy and tax implications of sale vs. hold
GST registration and margin scheme considerations

Our role complements your existing advisory relationships—it doesn't replace them.

FAQ

Common Questions

What Investors Ask

What's the minimum investment?

We typically work on projects requiring $500,000 or more in investor equity. This varies based on project type and whether finance is used.

Do I need development experience?

No. Our role is to bring the development expertise. What we look for is investors who understand property fundamentals and can commit capital for the project duration.

How involved do I need to be?

You stay informed, not consumed. Our secure investor portal gives you 24/7 access to project progress, updates, documentation, and direct communication with your team. Most investors spend a few hours per month on project matters.

What if the project doesn't proceed?

If feasibility doesn't support proceeding, you walk away with the documentation we've prepared. Where we've used option structures, your capital at risk is limited to option fee and feasibility costs.

Can I use finance?

Yes, most investors use a combination of equity and debt. Development finance typically requires 30-40% equity, though this varies by lender and project type.

What's my exit strategy?

You decide. Options include selling the completed asset, holding and leasing, refinancing to extract equity, or a combination. We help coordinate whichever exit suits your objectives.

Do you hold Professional Indemnity insurance?

Yes. We maintain Professional Indemnity insurance appropriate to our services. Details are provided during our initial discussions.

Can you also build the project?

For select projects, we can deliver construction through our in-house building capability—providing end-to-end delivery. Available by arrangement.
